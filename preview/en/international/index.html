What it actually is

An international transfer is not a long-haul taxi. It is an agreed chain: a car in Brazil, a border crossing under the rules of the specific country, a car and driver on the other side (or a single car for the whole route — where allowed), passenger documents, timing, and a fallback scenario.

What is agreed before departure

Not "as it happens on the road".

Documents and crossing format

We need passenger passports and understanding of the trip purpose (tourism / business / personal). On that basis we send the exact list of what to carry and the format of the specific crossing.

Car on the other side

For most Brazil–Mercosur pairs we can continue with one car (with the right paperwork). On some routes a handover to a local car right after the crossing is faster and more economical. Decided ahead of time.

Timing and overnight stops

On long legs an overnight stop is built in at an agreed point (hotel, apartment — by your preference). It is not "stopping wherever we end up", it is part of the plan.

Fallback scenario

For border queues, road repairs, weather or rule changes — a plan B is agreed in advance: alternative crossing, extra overnight, schedule shift.

Case-by-case, not mass

International legs are built per request.

These are not "press the button and go" routes. For each request we prepare the plan: points, documents, vehicle, driver, crossing, fallback. Reach out early — there will be time to do the trip properly.
