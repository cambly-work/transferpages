Glossary

Brazilian terms
you'll run into.

NF-e, CNPJ, Pix, DPVAT, CNH, LGPD — each in a single line.

NF-e
Nota Fiscal Eletrônica — mandatory electronic invoice in Brazil. Issued against the client's CNPJ. For accounting, it's the primary record for transfer expenses.
CNPJ
Cadastro Nacional da Pessoa Jurídica — corporate tax ID in Brazil. Without a CNPJ you cannot receive an NF-e.
CPF
Cadastro de Pessoas Físicas — personal tax ID. Needed for sizeable cashless payments.
Pix
Brazil's instant interbank payment. 24/7, fee-free for individuals. The fastest way to pay for a one-off transfer.
DPVAT
Mandatory passenger insurance on Brazilian ground transport. Covers basic injury/death scenarios. Applies automatically.
CNH
Carteira Nacional de Habilitação — Brazilian driver's licence. For passenger transport, category D or B + EAR is required.
BR-101
Coastal federal highway along Brazil's east. Main southern artery: SP → Curitiba → Florianópolis → Porto Alegre.
BR-116
Brazil's longest federal highway, from Fortaleza to Jaguarão (Uruguay border). The main business corridor.
LGPD
Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (Law 13.709/2018) — Brazil's GDPR-equivalent. Governs personal data processing. Supervised by ANPD.
ANPD
Autoridade Nacional de Proteção de Dados — Brazil's data protection regulator. Receives complaints if LGPD rights are violated.
CDC
Código de Defesa do Consumidor — Brazil's consumer protection code. Governs claims, cancellations and compensation in B2C.
Mercosur / Mercosul
Economic bloc: Brazil, Argentina, Uruguay, Paraguay. Between members there's a simplified customs regime for passengers.
Chuí
Land border town between Brazil and Uruguay. The main road crossing from southern Brazil into Mercosur.
